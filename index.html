What is Artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence is the ability of a computer program or machine to think and learn

Origins of A.I

The ‘beginning’ of modern A.I can be traced back to 1956, at Dartmouth College where the term ‘A.I’ was coined by John McCarthy.

Importance of A.I

Let’s answer the big question: What is the importance of A.I? Why do we even care?

Human-like capabilities

A.I is getting better and better at doing the normal human capabilities- understanding, reasoning, planning, communication and perception.

Expandability

A.I just like any computer, can be increased in power and numbers. The world’s data consumption and production rate is increasing so much that humans simply won’t suffice, which is why A.I is of great use.

Usage of A.I in real life

Artificial intelligence has been used in many places around the Globe, here are a few

Online Shopping and Advertising

A.I is being widely used to provide personalized recommendations to people based on the data from their previous searches and purchaces or other online behavior. Amazon is the most popular for this.

Web search

Search engines learn from the vast input of data, provided by their users to provide relevant search results.

Health

Researchers use A.I to analyse large quantities of data from hospitals to discover patterns that could lead to new discoveries in medicine.

Language Translation

A.I has been used numerable times in the translation of written or spoken text. This brings rise to functions like automated subtitling. Companies like Netflix use this in movies and shows.

Cars

Many companies like Tesla are developing self-driving cars which rely on sensors and data from their drivers. They continously get better as they get more customers.

A.I Against COVID-19

A.I has been widely used in thermal imaging in airports . In medicine, it can help recognize infection from computerised tomography lung scans. It has also been used to provide data to track the spread of the disease.

Chatbots

A Chatbot is a software that is used to replicate an on-line chat conversation using text or text-to-speech. This is widely used as a replacement for customer-service in large websites since it is cheaper.
